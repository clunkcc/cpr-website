<h2> Course Details </h2>

This course provides the skills and knowledge required to perform cardiopulmonary resuscitation (CPR) in line with first aid guidelines determined by the Australian Resuscitation Council (ARC) and other Australian national peak clinical bodies.

This course applies to all persons who may be required to provide a cardiopulmonary resuscitation (CPR) response in a range of situations, including community and workplace settings.

This course is aimed at early childhood workers and educators who work with school age children in and outside school hours care and vacation programs, new parents, school leavers and mature age persons who are looking for work or who are existing workers seeking to renew their qualifications.

It is recommended, by the Australian Resuscitation Council, that a CPR refresher program is undertaken every 12 months.

Specific licensing/regulatory requirements relating to this competency, including requirements for refresher training should be obtained from the relevant national/state/territory Work Health and Safety Regulatory Authorities.

- Reading and writing - A learner will be able to read, comprehend and write a range of texts within a variety of contexts.
- Oral communication - A learner will be able to use and respond to spoken language including some unfamiliar material within a variety of contexts.
- Digital literacy - A learner will be able to use a variety of digital devices and their applications/programs/software within a variety of contexts.

Further information about this course including Elements and Performance Criteria can be found at www.training.gov.au.


<h2> Location and types of Training </h2>

I offer both blended and face-to-face delivery for this course. The location is determined by students needs, however it is primarily delivered in community spaces near Brunswick, Victoria.


<h5>Blended training</h5>

This course can be taught as a blended option where theory is completed online before the course and we deliver the practical in-person.

<h5>Full face to face training</h5>

All training including theory and practical is conducted in-person with no pre-course learning.

<h2>Prerequisites</h2>

There are no prerequisites or age restrictions for this course. If student is under 18 years old at the time of training a [Parent / Guardian Consent form](https://www.bwfat.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2026/01/BW-Parent_Guardian-Consent-Form-v3.0.pdf) needs to be downloaded, printed and signed by the Parent / Guardian and brought to the course by the student.

<h2>Statement of Attainment</h2>


On successful completion of the course and verification of your USI Number, a nationally-recognised Statement of Attainment will be issued from my training partner – Business Wise First Aid Training (RTO 45193), showing you have fulfilled the requirements of HLTAID009 Provide cardiopulmonary resuscitation.

<h2>What should I wear?</h2>

It is a requirement of this course that a practical demonstration of CPR is performed for 2 minutes on the floor, so please, wear clothes that will enable you to complete this requirement.

<h2>Course Duration</h2>

**Blended option** (theory is completed online before attending the course and the practical training on the day of the course)

The online pre-course theory component on average takes students up to 2 hours to complete, some students take more time, some less time.

2 hours is allowed for the face to face or classroom component, but if the group is experienced in CPR this could take less time.

**Full Face to face option** (no pre-course learning, all training and assessment on the day of the course)

This option is only for people who have a current HLTAID009 Provide cardiopulmonary resuscitation qualification who are looking to renew their qualification, duration is up to 4 hours.

<h2>Pre-Course Learning</h2>

Once you have registered for your course, a link will be emailed to you to activate your account and access the online pre-course theory. This must be completed prior to attending the face-to-face component of the course.

There is no limit to the number of attempts at the online quizzes and no fee for additional attempts.  

Students must have a digital device i.e. computer, tablet or smart phone with access to the internet to complete the pre-course learning online.

<h2>Assessment Methods</h2>

You are assessed using scenarios and simulations. The trainer will observe these as you demonstrate the required skills and knowledge.

The pre-course online learning assessment comprises multiple choice, true or false as well as click and drag questions.
